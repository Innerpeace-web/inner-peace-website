01
· Executive Summary
A trusted local care business, ready for a website to match
Since 2022, Inner Peace In-Home Nursing & Care has built a genuine, compassionate reputation across East Gippsland — Bairnsdale, Lakes Entrance, Paynesville, Bruthen and the surrounding towns. The current website communicates that care clearly, but its presentation, structure and staff tooling no longer reflect the quality of the service behind it. This proposal sets out a focused plan to close that gap.
Where the site is strong today
Clear, well-described services
Personal, Nursing, Social and Dementia Care are each explained in plain, reassuring language.
A warm, human brand voice
The tone is empathetic and local — exactly what families look for when choosing in-home care.
Authentic client testimonials
Real reviews already speak to the impact on wellbeing and independence.
Credible, trustworthy operator
Qualified, insured carers and an established booking process show a serious, safe provider.
Where there is room to grow
A dated visual presentation
The template look and dense text blocks undersell a premium, caring service.
An unfocused homepage & navigation
Visitors aren't guided to one clear next step, and service links follow inconsistent paths.
A limited staff login
The existing login isn't yet a true resource hub for policies, training and forms.
Under-used conversion signals
Insurance, qualifications and reviews exist but aren't placed where they build trust fastest.
Why a redesign matters here.
Choosing in-home care is an emotional, high-trust decision — often made by an adult child researching on a phone, late at night. A modern, consistent, mobile-first website removes doubt and makes the next step obvious, which lifts enquiries. A dedicated staff portal does the same job internally: it centralises resources, speeds up onboarding, and supports compliance — freeing your team to focus on clients rather than chasing documents.

03
· Opportunities for Improvement
Six priority areas to address
These are the highest-impact opportunities identified during the review. None reflect a failure of the current site — they are the natural next step for a business that has outgrown its original online presence.
Outdated visual design
The current layout relies on a dated template aesthetic with heavy text blocks and small imagery. First impressions form in seconds, and the visuals currently undersell the warmth and quality of the care provided.
Impact: Brand perception
Weak homepage structure
The homepage opens with a video and long paragraphs but no clear visual hierarchy or single primary action. Visitors aren't guided toward "book a consultation," so intent leaks away before they convert.
Impact: Conversion
Navigation complexity
Menus repeat and overlap, "Login" sits inside the public navigation, and services are reached through two different URL patterns. This makes the site harder to scan and weakens its structure for search engines.
Impact: Usability
Branding inconsistencies
Naming shifts between "Inner Peace Vision" and "In-Home Nursing & Care," the footer still reads © 2023, and a broken company link points to an incomplete address. Small details like these quietly erode trust.
Impact: Credibility
Staff portal limitations
A staff login already exists, but it isn't yet a genuine resource centre. Policies, training, forms and announcements live in scattered places, making onboarding and day-to-day reference slower than they should be.
Impact: Operations
Conversion optimisation
Phone and booking options exist but aren't consistently visible, testimonials are tucked away, and trust signals are spread thin. Clear, repeated calls to action and visible proof will turn more visitors into enquiries.
Impact: Lead generation

A homepage that guides, not just informs
The new homepage tells a clear story from top to bottom: who you are, what you offer, why you're trusted, proof, where you serve, and how to take the next step. Below is the recommended section order, with the purpose of each.

Section 02

Four clean cards — Personal, Nursing, Social and Dementia Care — each with an icon, a one-line summary and a "Learn more" link. This lets every visitor self-select the support they need and routes them straight to the right service page.

· Recommended Design Direction
A calm, modern, premium healthcare aesthetic
The visual direction should feel as reassuring as the care itself — soft, spacious and professional. Light, natural colour; generous whitespace; refined typography; and warm, candid imagery of real people and places.
Modern healthcare aesthetic
Soft teal, gentle blue and sage greens on white — a palette that reads clean, clinical and caring without feeling cold.
More whitespace
Room around every element reduces overwhelm, improves readability, and signals a calm, considered, premium service.
Better typography
A warm, editorial serif for headings paired with a clean, friendly sans for body — clear hierarchy, easy reading at any age.
Professional imagery
Authentic, natural photography of carers, clients and East Gippsland — real warmth, never generic stock.
Improved trust signals
Qualifications, insurance, reviews and local credentials surfaced clearly and consistently across the site.
Better mobile experience
Designed mobile-first, with tap-friendly buttons and a click-to-call number always within reach.

10
· Conclusion
A strong business, ready for a website to match
Inner Peace In-Home Nursing & Care has already done the hardest part: it has earned genuine trust, delivered real care, and built a warm, credible reputation across East Gippsland.
The current website holds strong foundations — clear services, an authentic voice, real testimonials and valuable content. What it now needs is a presentation that lives up to the quality of the service behind it. A modern, mobile-first redesign will make the brand feel as caring and professional as the team itself, guide more families toward making an enquiry, and remove the small inconsistencies that quietly undermine confidence today.
Paired with a dedicated staff portal, the overhaul also strengthens the business from the inside — centralising resources, simplifying onboarding, supporting compliance, and keeping the whole team informed.
The recommendation is clear: build on what already works, and give Inner Peace the modern, trusted online presence and team hub it deserves.

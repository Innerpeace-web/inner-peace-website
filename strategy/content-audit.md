## 2. Content Quality Audit

### 2a. Tone & voice consistency
- **Core voice is strong and consistent** across the marketing pages: warm, local, dignity-centred, anti-institutional. The signature phrases recur intentionally — "cherishing life's simple pleasures … a refreshing day at the local pool … exploring hidden treasures at the op shop" (`/`, `/about-us/`) and "goes beyond the confines of traditional residential care" (`/`, `/about-us/`). Framework alignment: the prescribed tone is "gentle & human," "confident, never clinical-cold."
- **Two distinct voice eras coexist.** The 2023-built service/area pages are dense, adjective-heavy and salesy ("unparalleled and compassionate," "redefines care standards," "top-notch care" — `/area-served/…-in-bairnsdale/`). The 2026-built `/dementia-care/` is markedly cleaner and more clinical-professional ("Clear, practical dementia support with a human centre"; "The page has been simplified so visitors can quickly understand…"). The framework's prescribed register matches the newer page, not the older corpus — the dementia page is the **tone benchmark** to roll out.
- **Brand-name drift** breaks voice authority: "Inner Peace In-Home Nursing & Care" (canonical) vs **"Inner Peace Vision"** (`team-members` image alt on `/`; privacy notice on `/dementia-care/`: "designated Inner Peace Vision email addresses") vs **"Inner Peace Services"** (`/dementia-care/` consent line; "Inner Peace Services Pty Ltd" in `/privacy-policy/`). Framework principle: a single confident brand identity (`_facts.md §10 D3`). **Recommendation:** lock one public-facing name; reserve "Inner Peace Services Pty Ltd" for legal copy only.

### 2b. Reading level & clarity
- **Service detail pages** (`/personal-care/`, `/social-care/`, `/nursing-care/`) are at an appropriate, scannable reading level with short itemised blocks — well matched to the framework's target reader ("an adult child researching on a phone, late at night") and "readable-at-any-age scale."
- **`/area-served/` town pages over-write** — three paragraphs of superlatives ("unparalleled," "meticulously tailored," "genuinely transforms lives") with low information density. This raises reading effort while saying little that is town-specific. Framework: clarity and "understands what you do … in five seconds."
- **`/in-home-nursing-care-benefits/`** is comprehensive (15 benefits) but entirely generic — no Inner-Peace specifics, no East Gippsland references, no Annette/RN credibility. It reads like stock home-care copy. Framework: "real warmth, never generic."
- **Typos undermine professionalism on careers/legal surfaces:** "SCREENING QUESTIONAIRRE" (`/interview-form/` H1), "Australia Securities and Investments Commision" (`/terms-conditions/`), legacy privacy text referencing "vehicle" research (`/privacy-policy/`, a copy-paste artefact the new 2026 policy explicitly disowns: "No vehicle research … of any kind is collected or relevant to our services").

## 4. Content Consolidation Opportunities

### 4a. Six near-identical area pages = duplicate-content risk
**Evidence:** `/area-served/…-in-bairnsdale/`, `-bruthen/`, `-east-gippsland/`, `-lakes-entrance/`, `-lindenow-south/`, `-paynesville/` are template clones — the three-paragraph body is identical with only the town name string-swapped, all share the same hero `image-09-1536x1024-1`, the same `Book an appointment` CTA, and the same 4-card service grid (`_facts.md §10 D12`). Two of them carry the **wrong** meta description (the Social Care one).
**Framework:** the prescribed sitemap folds area-served into the **Resources** hub; the build values quality over duplicate thin pages.
**Merge strategy:**
1. Build one strong **Service Area** page (under Resources) with a real East-Gippsland map + the town list (Bairnsdale · Bruthen · East Gippsland · Lakes Entrance · Lindenow South · Paynesville · "towns in between") — matching homepage section (E).
2. Retain at most the **two highest-value** town pages where genuinely localised content can be written (Bairnsdale — the brokerage-partner town per `/contact-us/`; and the broad "East Gippsland" term), each rewritten with unique, town-specific copy.
3. 301-redirect the remaining thin town pages to the Service Area page to preserve any equity and remove duplication. Fix the mismatched meta descriptions before any are kept.

### 4b. "Three services" vs four mismatch
**Evidence:** `/in-home-nursing-care-services/` states "Explore our **three** services below" then renders **four** cards — Personal, Social, Nursing, **Dementia** (`_facts.md §10 D6`). Compounding this, `/foot-care/` exists as a fifth (stub) service and shows only **three** sibling cards (omitting Dementia).
**Framework:** "A visitor understands what you do … in five seconds"; one consistent path to every service.
**Consolidation:** Decide the canonical service set. The framework's prescribed `Services` menu = **Personal · Nursing · Social · Dementia** (four). Recommendation: adopt those four, fix the hub copy to "four services," and either (a) build out `/foot-care/` into real content or (b) retire it (currently "Coming Soon!", **D7**) — do not leave an indexable stub that contradicts the count.

### 4c. Dead `/home-care-services/` path
**Evidence:** `/home-care-services/` returns **404** ("This page could not be found!", `statusCode 404`), yet `/` and `/about-us/` both hyperlink the words "personalised care" to its child `/home-care-services/personal-care/` (`_facts.md §10 D1`).
**Framework:** "predictable URLs"; "one consistent path to every service."
**Consolidation:** Repoint both in-text "personalised care" links to the live `/in-home-nursing-care-services/personal-care/` and 301-redirect the entire `/home-care-services/*` pattern to `/in-home-nursing-care-services/*`. This eliminates the broken link from the two highest-authority pages and unifies the URL namespace (also supports fixing **D2**).

### 4d. Overloaded `/contact-us/` → split into purpose-built pages
**Evidence:** `/contact-us/` carries five distinct content blocks — Urgent Assistance, Care Inquiry, **Brokerage Services**, **Employment Opportunities** + **Join Our Team** (DISC/Interview links), and the contact form.
**Framework:** "single primary action per section"; Resources consolidates careers entry.
**Consolidation:** Keep Contact focused on contact + urgent help; migrate Employment/Join-Our-Team to the new Careers page (§3) and brokerage/self-management detail to the new Funding page (§3), each linked from Contact.

### 4e. Duplicated brand intro across `/` and `/about-us/`
**Evidence:** the four-paragraph intro ("we are committed to … op shop") appears verbatim on both `/` and `/about-us/`.
**Framework:** avoid duplicate indexable bodies; let each page do one job.
**Consolidation:** Keep the full narrative on `/about-us/`; on `/` reduce it to a confident hero headline + a single trust cue ("Caring for East Gippsland since 2022"), per homepage section (A).

## 5. Content Audit Summary

- **Strengths to preserve (crawl-verified):** rich, specific service task/activity lists (`/personal-care/`, `/social-care/`, `/nursing-care/`); strong founder credibility (`/about-us/`); the modern, clean `/dementia-care/` build (the tone + structure benchmark); genuine testimonials (`/`); a robust, current legal/privacy baseline citing the Aged Care Act 2024 and SaH funding (`/privacy-policy/`).
- **Biggest content liabilities:** six near-duplicate area pages (**D12**), the "Coming Soon!" foot-care stub (**D7**), buried/unattributed testimonials (**D8**), buried trust copy (**D10**), and the dead `/home-care-services/` links from `/` and `/about-us/` (**D1**).
- **Top missing content (highest conversion value):** a reader-facing **Funding (SaH / Home Care Packages)** page, a **Careers** page, a **Testimonials** page, an **FAQ**, and a **Resources** hub — every one of these has source facts already scattered in the crawl, so consolidation (not invention) is the path. All trace to the framework's prescribed sitemap and conversion model (`_facts.md §12`).
